The **Timing Check Condition** in a Smart Flow allows you to evaluate the timing of an event or action to determine whether to proceed with a specific step in the flow.

###  **Topics covered:**

- [How to Configure Timing Check Condition](#how-to-configure-timing-check-condition)

- [Practical Example](#practical-example)

###  How to Configure Timing Check Condition

When setting up a Smart Flow, choose the **Timing Check** condition.

![mceclip0.png](./images/mceclip0-5.png)

After making your selection, you will need to configure the **Timing Check condition** by entering the following details:

- **Name:** Assign a clear and descriptive title for the condition that indicates its purpose within the flow.

- **Description:** Provide a brief explanation of what this condition does and why itâ€™s important in the context of the flow.

- **Availability Schedule:** Define the specific time frames during which the condition will be evaluated.Once the configuration is completed, hit **Save**.

![mceclip1.png](./images/mceclip1-5.png)

###  Practical Example

- The **Timing Check** condition can be used to verify if a contact was created during the morning shift (9 AM - 5 PM). If it was, it will assign the contact to the team working during that shift. If not, it checks for availability during the evening shift and routes the contact accordingly.
